=== About Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) ===

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a long-term condition where the kidneys don't work as well as they should. The kidneys are responsible for filtering waste and excess fluids from your blood, which are then removed from your body through urine. When kidney function declines, dangerous levels of waste and fluid can build up in your body.

CKD is classified into five stages based on the severity of kidney damage:
- Stage 1: Kidney damage with normal or increased GFR (≥90 mL/min)
- Stage 2: Mild decrease in GFR (60-89 mL/min)
- Stage 3: Moderate decrease in GFR (30-59 mL/min)
- Stage 4: Severe decrease in GFR (15-29 mL/min)
- Stage 5: Kidney failure (GFR <15 mL/min or dialysis)

=== Common Symptoms of CKD ===

Early-stage CKD often has no symptoms. As the disease progresses, symptoms may include:
- Fatigue and weakness
- Swelling in legs, ankles, or feet (edema)
- Shortness of breath
- Nausea and vomiting
- Loss of appetite
- Changes in urine output
- Difficulty sleeping
- Muscle cramps
- Persistent itching
- High blood pressure
- Decreased mental sharpness
- Chest pain (if fluid builds up around the heart)
- Foamy urine indicating protein in urine

=== Risk Factors for CKD ===

Several factors increase the risk of developing chronic kidney disease:
- Diabetes (both Type 1 and Type 2)
- High blood pressure (hypertension)
- Heart disease and cardiovascular conditions
- Family history of kidney disease
- Age (risk increases after 60)
- Obesity and being overweight
- Smoking
- Prolonged use of certain medications (NSAIDs, certain antibiotics)
- Abnormal kidney structure
- Previous acute kidney injury
- Certain ethnic backgrounds (African American, Native American, Asian American)

=== Important Medical Parameters for CKD ===

Our AI model evaluates several key medical parameters to predict CKD risk:

1. **Blood Pressure (BP)**: Normal is around 120/80 mmHg. High blood pressure can damage kidneys.

2. **Specific Gravity (SG)**: Measures urine concentration. Normal range: 1.005-1.030. Abnormal values may indicate kidney problems.

3. **Albumin**: Protein in urine. Normal is negative. Presence indicates kidney damage.

4. **Blood Sugar**: Normal fasting: 70-100 mg/dL. High levels (diabetes) can damage kidneys.

5. **Blood Urea (BU)**: Waste product. Normal: 7-20 mg/dL. High levels indicate poor kidney function.

6. **Serum Creatinine (SC)**: Waste product from muscles. Normal: 0.6-1.2 mg/dL (men), 0.5-1.1 mg/dL (women). High levels indicate kidney problems.

7. **Hemoglobin (Hemo)**: Protein in red blood cells. Normal: 13.5-17.5 g/dL (men), 12-15.5 g/dL (women). Low levels common in CKD.

8. **Packed Cell Volume (PCV)**: Percentage of red blood cells. Normal: 38-50% (men), 36-44% (women). Low in CKD.

9. **White Blood Cell Count (WBC)**: Normal: 4,000-11,000 cells/µL. High may indicate infection or inflammation.

10. **Red Blood Cell Count (RBC)**: Normal: 4.5-5.5 million cells/µL (men), 4.0-5.0 million cells/µL (women). Low in CKD.

11. **Hypertension**: High blood pressure history increases CKD risk significantly.

12. **Diabetes Mellitus**: Having diabetes is a major risk factor for CKD.

13. **Coronary Artery Disease**: Heart disease is linked to kidney disease.

14. **Appetite**: Loss of appetite is common in advanced CKD.

15. **Pedal Edema**: Swelling in feet/ankles indicates fluid retention.

16. **Anemia**: Common in CKD due to reduced erythropoietin production.

=== Treatment and Management of CKD ===

While CKD cannot be cured in most cases, its progression can be slowed:

**Medical Treatment:**
- Blood pressure medications (ACE inhibitors, ARBs)
- Diabetes management medications
- Medications to lower cholesterol
- Medications to treat anemia
- Medications to relieve swelling
- Phosphate binders
- Vitamin D supplements

**Lifestyle Changes:**
- Follow a kidney-friendly diet (low sodium, controlled protein)
- Regular exercise (as approved by doctor)
- Maintain healthy weight
- Quit smoking
- Limit alcohol consumption
- Stay hydrated (as per doctor's advice)
- Manage underlying conditions (diabetes, hypertension)

**Advanced Treatment:**
- Dialysis (hemodialysis or peritoneal dialysis) for Stage 5 CKD
- Kidney transplant for end-stage kidney disease

=== Prevention of CKD ===

Steps to prevent or reduce risk of CKD:
1. Control blood sugar if you have diabetes
2. Maintain healthy blood pressure
3. Maintain healthy weight
4. Eat a balanced, kidney-friendly diet
5. Don't smoke
6. Limit alcohol
7. Exercise regularly (30 minutes most days)
8. Get regular health checkups
9. Drink plenty of water unless advised otherwise
10. Avoid overuse of NSAIDs and other harmful medications
11. Manage stress
12. Get adequate sleep
